# B2B SaaS Cold Outreach Playbook: 2026 Strategy

## 1. Core Outreach Strategy (The Step-by-Step Guide)

To build a high-performing pipeline, follow this 3-pillar framework derived from top practitioners:

* **Mobile-First Email Formatting**: Write emails that are under 30 words and use 3 sentences max. If it requires scrolling on a phone, it's too long.
    * *(Source: Will Allred, LinkedIn Post, 12.03.2024 - [Post Link](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/willallred_cold-email-tips-activity-7173294829148303361-LhA_))*
* **The Permission-Based Opener (PBO)**: When cold calling, start by acknowledging the interruption. 
    * *Example*: "I know I caught you in the middle of something, do you have 30 seconds for me to tell you why I called?"
    * *(Source: Nick Cegelski, 30MPC Podcast Episode, 15.01.2024 - [Link](https://www.30mpc.com/podcast))*
* **Relevant, Not Just Personalized**: Don't mention their hobbies. Mention a "Trigger Event" like a recent company hire or a new technology they started using.
    * *(Source: Jed Mahrle, Practical Prospecting Newsletter, 22.02.2024 - [Post Link](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jed-mahrle_personalization-vs-relevance-activity-7166412938148843520-Xq8k))*

## 2. Where Experts Disagree

### A. High Volume vs. Low Volume
* **What Justin Michael Recommends**: Suggests using AI and automation to send 100+ "Spear" emails daily to maximize the "math of sales."
* **What Josh Braun Recommends**: Suggests sending only 5-10 highly researched, handcrafted emails per day to avoid being marked as spam.
* **My Stance**: I side with **Josh Braun**. In B2B SaaS, the market is small. If you burn your reputation with 100 prospects in one week using automation, you lose your "addressable market" quickly.

### B. Video Prospecting as the "First Touch"
* **What Becc Holland Recommends**: Believes a personalized video should be the very first message a prospect receives to show you did the work.
* **What Charlotte Johnson Recommends**: Recommends sending a text-based email or LinkedIn message first to "warm up" the lead before sending a video link.
* **My Stance**: I side with **Charlotte Johnson**. Most corporate firewalls block video links from unknown senders. You must establish human trust via text first.

### C. The Use of Rapport (Small Talk)
* **What Jason Bay Recommends**: Suggests finding one piece of "common ground" (like a shared city) to ease into the conversation.
* **What Belal Batrawy Recommends**: Argues for "Death to Fluff"—get straight to the business problem in the first 2 seconds.
* **My Stance**: I side with **Belal Batrawy**. Modern executives are time-poor. "How are you doing?" is a red flag that identifies you as a salesperson immediately.

## 3. What I Rejected and Why

1.  **Idea**: Using "Bump" emails (e.g., "Just circling back on this").
    * **Reasoning**: I rejected this because it adds zero value to the prospect. If the first email didn't work, a "circle back" email just adds to their inbox noise. I prefer the "Value-Added Follow-up" approach.
2.  **Idea**: Personal Cell Phone Cold Calling.
    * **Reasoning**: While tools exist to find cell numbers, I rejected this as a core strategy because it feels intrusive in 2026. If a prospect hasn't opted in, a cell call can damage the brand before the conversation starts.

---

## 4. My Original Idea: The "Pre-Meeting Prep" Video
**The Concept**: After a meeting is booked, send a 15-second screen-share video showing you have already started researching their specific software dashboard or website.
* **Why it works**: Most SaaS meetings have a 20-30% "No-Show" rate. By showing that you have already put in "pre-work" *before* the meeting, the prospect feels a social obligation to show up, drastically increasing conversion rates.

---

## 5. Weaknesses of this Playbook
* **Untested in Non-Tech Industries**: This playbook is optimized for SaaS. It might fail in traditional industries like Manufacturing or Healthcare where email isn't the primary communication tool.
* **Assumption of Technical Literacy**: This strategy assumes the prospect is active on LinkedIn. If the prospect doesn't have a LinkedIn presence, 40% of this playbook's "touches" are useless.
